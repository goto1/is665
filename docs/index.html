Investments by major companies

Findings

In total, those major companies were responsible for around $14B in investments. That's about 3.3% of the total $428.4B invested during those seven years.

The top 3 companies that invested the most are Google, Samsung, and Microsoft.

Comparison

Google invested $11B, or 78.6% out of the $14B
Samsung invested $1.2B, or 8.6% out of the $14B
Microsoft invested $1.1B, or 7.7% out of the $14B
Apple invested $377M, or 2.7% out of the $14B
HTC invested $184M, or 1.31% out of the $14B
LG invested $32M, or 0.22% out of the $14B

Acquisitions by major companies

Acquisitions by major companies

Overview

The amount of money spent on acquisitions between 2007 and 2014 was $2.8 trillion. Apple, Google, HTC, LG, Microsoft, and Samsung together made up about 21% of that sum, which is about $60.4B.

Comparison

Google spent $25.5B for acquisitions, with top 3 markets of mobile, advertising, and home automation
Microsoft spent $22.7B for acquisitions, with top 3 markets of mobile, curated web, and games
Apple spent $8.2B for acquisitions, with top 3 markets of semiconductors, hardware + software, and advertising
Samsung spent $577M for acquisitions in the home automation market
LG spent $200M for acquisitions
HTC spent $31.5M for acquisitions in the mobile and software markets
